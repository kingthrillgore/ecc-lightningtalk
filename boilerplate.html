A decade of psuedo-trust

  • We rely on strong Pseudo-Random Number Generators (pRNGs) to provision keys for encryption
  • pRNG sources (/dev/random, memory entropy) are considerably unreliable and predictable when compared to true entropy sources
  • Theoretically, we could provide possible sequences for the exhaustive task of brute-forcing keys forged via pRNG

What should I do?

  • Use ECC algorithms that are public and have been subject to intense peer review
  • DSCG 142 A is the official algorithm adopted by OpenSSL, GPG, and the OpenBSD Crypto stack
  • The OpenBSD Crypto mailing list is a good source to see the inner workings of algorithm peer reviews
